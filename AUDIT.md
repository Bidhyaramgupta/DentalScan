# AUDIT

DentalScan’s landing page is clear and credible: the value proposition is easy to understand, the 3-step workflow is simple, and the scan examples help set expectations. The strongest part is that the product explains the full path from patient photo capture to AI analysis and claim prep in one place. That said, the discovery-to-scan handoff could be smoother. As a first-time user, I could reach the main site and the dashboard organization selector, but the actual patient capture flow appears to depend on a secure patient link rather than an openly accessible demo. If that is intentional, the site should make the next step more explicit with a true interactive demo, sample scan walkthrough, or “see capture flow” preview.

From a UX perspective, the 5-angle scan will likely succeed or fail based on guidance quality. Patients need stronger real-time coaching: a centered mouth guide, distance feedback (“move closer / farther”), angle-specific overlays, lighting warnings, blur detection, and a progress stepper that clearly shows Front, Left, Right, Upper, and Lower. A short example image or animation before each angle would reduce hesitation.

The biggest technical risk on mobile is camera instability. Hand shake, autofocus hunting, low light, exposure shifts, motion blur, and awkward upper/lower mouth positioning can reduce image quality fast. On lower-end phones, live camera preview plus overlays can also create frame drops or heat. I would add lightweight real-time quality checks, stability scoring before capture, automatic retake prompts, and device-specific fallbacks for older mobile browsers. The core product idea is strong; the biggest opportunity is making capture feel guided, forgiving, and confidence-building.
